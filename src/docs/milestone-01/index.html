Overview

TransKnowledge is a carefully crafted web application that seeks to centralize information and resources for transgender people’s wellbeing. Misinformation about transitioning is widespread, especially given how many trans communities are private for safety reasons. For trans people who are currently in the closet or have few real life trans peers, finding reliable information about transitioning is a daunting task, much less finding a safe community. Featuring not only a user-updated repository of trans information and resources, but a trans law heatmap, trans news feed, and moderated community forum, any information a trans person needs to thrive and make educated decisions can be found. TransKnowledge seeks to serve all members of the trans community, regardless of age, ability, or technological literacy, by having a simple-to-use, minimalist interface and clear focus points. Through this process, trans people of all stripes can be sure they are getting reliable information without scouring the internet and can find community where one may not exist in their real-world location.

Application Parts

There are four foundational elements that make up the TransKnowledge application. First is the Trans Map, which provides users with an easily-understood map interface, showing the current legal situation for trans people in all states. Not only is more information about each state provided, but a route mode allows mapping between areas using trans friendly states, ideal for making road trips that avoid danger as much as possible.

The Trans News page aggregates and filters information from all across the political spectrum, allowing each member to stay informed on latest news without needlessly subjecting themselves to hateful rhetoric. It also features an RSS feed for delivering information seamlessly and the ability to add other news feeds as desired. This process exemplifies the project’s mission of finding trustworthy information on trans issues and making it as accessible as possible to digest, while still providing choice in the user’s desired sources of information.

Users can ask questions and seek reassurance with other members of the community on the Community Forums page helping to reduce the effects of isolation, especially with people who are still in the closet. Incorporating an accessible moderation tool of reporting low-quality or hateful posts in its interconnected platform while respecting anonymity fosters a safe and respectful community.

The Resources page is an archive of information, divided into subject matter of most interest to a wide variety of trans people, such as accessing medical information and understanding legal name and gender changes. Through a moderated user submission form, the page can become a living document, able to change with new, reliable information while keeping demand on a single person to update it as minimal as possible.

Data Requirements

The purpose of TransKnowledge is to act as a data repository, carefully curated to maintain user safety and veracity, yet continuously updated with the latest information and maintaining accessibility to non-tech literate users. As such, the data sources were chosen to balance these characteristics as much as possible.

The foundation of TransKnowledge is built on user-sourced information, both through interpersonal interaction in community forums and in contributed updates to resources. The type of data is relatively simple, being entirely plaintext submitting in a particular form and not tracking any identifiable user data. However, it also requires moderation by humans involved with the project. This moderation is fundamental in keeping the site safe and alive, which demands the data are retrievable in a human-readable format and easily added to the final project if approved.

The Trans Map page requires GPS data for mapping routes between areas. This will be sourced from a preexisting service such as the Google Maps API, with specific demands that include avoiding certain destinations whenever possible. This routing capability makes it possible to take away the fear of long-distance trips and encourages mutual aid to places that are undergoing a shift to hateful policies.

The news feed requires automatic retrieval of the latest news articles from a large number of publications. This will most likely be possible through either API data or an RSS feed. However, in order to ensure that the users only see data pertaining to trans issues, it will be required to automatically filter for articles about trans issues. Some APIs, such as the New York Times, have tagging data set up already, but for others, it may be necessary to create a filter that searches articles for keywords pertaining to trans issues and selects the ones that meet a particular threshold. The creation of this filter will reduce the difficulty of researching and keeping up to date on trans news, allowing information to disseminate to more people.

Finally, the resources page acts as a database of reliable external resources for trans interests, centralizing information to a larger and less technologically savvy user base. Vital to the project is proper categorization, which can reduce the mental load on users and increase the number of resources feasible to keep at a time. Ease of updating will also ensure that the information is up to date and incorrect information is removed to prevent further misinformation within the community.

By combining crowdsourced information with focused web design elements, this platform balances stability with breadth, which encourages a degree of longevity that would not exist with a typical consolidated repository of information.

Wire Frames

The core design of TransKnowledge is based around minimalist elements to facilitate its role as a space of knowledge and for ease of use to serve all members of the trans community.

The Trans Map page features an interactive map of the United States and three optional floating boxes used for route input, details of the route chosen, and details of the state selected. By keeping the map as the central focus, it is simple for users to immediately understand and internalize the makeup of the United States concerning transgender laws. The optional boxes allow for more information only when needed.

The Trans News page is centered around news articles featured in a reader view, with an RSS feed and source selector off to the sides. Plenty of space is dedicated to the articles, as the user will spend the vast majority of their time solely reading them. The news filters are the secondary point of interest, which is why they are given a large sidebar off to the side.

The Community Forums feature a social media-like design of anonymous posts in a center column in chronological order, with replies and reports in floating boxes. By keeping the UI and minimalist, the design will be welcoming to those familiar and unfamiliar with social media alike. Likewise, the reply and report buttons are the only ones available to encourage positive interaction with other users and to help older or less technologically savvy users understand it.

Finally, the Resources page uses a hierarchical model, with the section selector as a sidebar and the resources given plenty of space in the middle. This type of layout should be familiar to users from features like the iOS settings page, encouraging interaction and keeping the page neatly ordered for ease of use. Given that most users will be reading resources more than they will be submitting new sources, that feature is given less prominence in the corner.

Real-World Connection

The unfortunate reality of information on trans issues is that it is dispersed and kept within communities, as a result of the everyday violence experienced by trans people. With TransKnowledge, isolated trans people can find support with one web application. A vital aspect is combining moderation with user-submitting content. This allows the Resources page to balance adapting new knowledge as needed with keeping out harmful false information and allows the Community Forums to prevent bad actors from causing harm without gatekeeping the space away from those who need it most. For instance, a trans person who learned of an online store that sold hormone replacement medication could check to see if there was any information about it on the site, as a number of similar stores do not sell what they claim to. Likewise, the Trans Map page provides a perspective on which states have the most and least legal protection for trans people, which gives actionable information on where users can go to be safe. Taken together, TransKnowledge would reduce the effects of misinformation that isolated trans people would be at risk of.

In addition, many trans people who are particularly at risk of isolation and misinformation are people unaccustomed to technology, given that a growing proportion of trans communities rely on online communication. The streamlined design of TransKnowledge is targeted to make less tech-literate people comfortable, allowing them access to information and community they might not otherwise have.

Integrative Experience

The creation of TransKnowledge requires skills obtained throughout the entirety of my university experience. As an Informatics & Social Thought and Political Economy (STPEC) double major, the focus of my education has been on understanding how to use and communicate information and understanding minority community interests. In particular, my GenEd course BIOLOGY105H: Biology of Social Issues and INFO348: Predictive Analytics in Python were instrumental in understanding how to find accurate information about minority communities and how to coalesce and communicate efficiently. One of the Integrative Experience requirements is interdisciplinary perspective-taking, which this project has in spades, from computer science skills in how data can be aggregated and distributed to STPEC skills in understanding and addressing the needs of a particular community.

In addition, my experience as a trans person who came out during my time at university has also informed the goals and structure of TransKnowledge. I was in a similar position as my intended user base. I had few real life trans relationships I could draw on for knowledge and being swarmed by misinformation. As such, I drew on what I needed most: information about trans laws in various states, a way to aggregate trans news articles without subjecting myself to hate speech, a community, and vetted resources. As such, this project acts as a culmination of my particular education and social experience in university.
